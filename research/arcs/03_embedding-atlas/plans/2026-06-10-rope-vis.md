# RoPE visualization arc — plan (scaffold)

**Status:** planned, not started. Queued behind the embedding-atlas arc's
first findings (user decision 2026-06-10: two arcs, embeddings first).

**Research question (one sentence):** What does Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct's rotary
positional encoding (RoPE) do to query/key vectors geometrically — and which
of its 64 frequency bands do trained attention heads actually use to carry
position vs content?

## Verified model facts the design rests on

From the cached `config.json` (HF snapshot `a09a3545…bc28`):
`rope_theta = 1,000,000.0`, `head_dim = 128` (3584 / 28 heads), 28 query
heads / 4 KV heads (GQA), `max_position_embeddings = 32768`.

RoPE rotates each consecutive pair of head dimensions `(2k, 2k+1)` by angle
`m * theta^(-2k/d)` at position `m` — so head_dim 128 gives **64 frequency
bands** with wavelengths `2*pi*theta^(2k/128)` spanning ~6.3 tokens (band 0)
up to ~6.3M tokens (band 63). With theta = 1e6, most high-`k` bands never
complete even a fraction of one rotation inside the 32k context — a key
visual: which bands are "fast clocks", which are effectively constant.

## Candidate experiments (ordered)

1. **Rotation-frequency spectrum (model-free).** Compute the 64 inverse
   frequencies from the config; plot wavelength vs band index, rotation angle
   vs position for representative bands, and the fraction of a full turn each
   band completes at context lengths 128 / 2k / 32k. Pure math from the
   config — no model load; establishes the geometric vocabulary
   ("fast/slow bands") every later figure uses.
2. **Same-token-different-position k-vector trace.** Hook `k_proj` (and apply
   the rotary transform as the attention module does), feed a prompt
   containing the same token at several positions, and visualize the k-vector
   differences band-by-band: positions differ ONLY by rotation, and the plot
   makes the per-band rotation visible on real vectors. Connects directly to
   the embedding-atlas arc: the pre-RoPE k is a linear image of the
   (position-free) residual stream.
3. **Head-wise band-usage map.** For each of the 28 query heads: the norm of
   the `q_proj`/`k_proj` weight slices per rotary band (which bands the head
   can even read), plus per-band variance of actual q/k activations over a
   probe corpus. Hypothesis to test: some heads concentrate weight in fast
   bands (positional heads), others in slow/never-rotating bands (semantic
   heads). [SPECULATION until measured.]
4. **QK phase-alignment / relative-offset curve.** For selected heads,
   compute the attention logit between a fixed query and the same key
   content placed at varying relative offsets; decompose the logit into
   per-band contributions. This is the "see the position-dependence" figure:
   RoPE makes `q . k` a function of relative offset, and the per-band
   decomposition shows which bands produce the oscillation vs the envelope.
5. **Trace-back into generation (stretch).** During a short generation,
   visualize per-head attention as a function of relative offset alongside
   band usage — the first step toward the "trace the thought process"
   framing, and the natural bridge to the llobotomy live-viz frontend.
