# Paper 2 — *Training is a multi-stage pipeline, not a single objective*

**Working title:** *Training is a multi-stage pipeline, not a single objective: pre-training data, scaling, optimization, distributed scaffolding, mixed precision, and the post-training stack of frontier LLMs, 2020–2026.*

**Thesis:** Modern frontier-LLM training is a six-stage pipeline (pre-training-data → scaling-and-optimization → distributed/mixed-precision scaffolding → SFT → preference-RL → reasoning-RL → adaptation/merging). Each stage's load-bearing variables — token count, mixture, parametrization, precision, batch size, KL constraint, verifier signal, merge geometry — are now characterized well enough to write down. The pipeline composes; pretending it is one objective produces persistently confused empirics (Kaplan-vs-Chinchilla, RLHF-vs-DPO-vs-PPO, "RLVR creates new capability"). We name the stages, formalize each, walk the lineages, and identify where the pipeline's open frontiers still are.

**Length target:** ~100 pages. Broadest scope of the five papers because the pipeline is genuinely six stages and each stage has its own canonical lineage.

**Audience:** ML researchers and engineers with graduate-level math who want one place to read the synthesized training-pipeline state of frontier LLMs. Not a tutorial; not a survey. A monograph. Paper 1's notation (residual stream, FFN, attention, RMSNorm) is assumed.

### §3 — Scaling laws (10 pages, anchor section)

**Scaling laws are a fitted functional form, not a literal compute recipe.** Kaplan 2020's three power laws (Eq. 1.1–1.3) and the joint $L(N, D)$ form (Eq. 1.5) are still authoritative; Kaplan's compute-allocation rule ($N \propto C^{0.73}$) was overturned by Chinchilla's matched-LR-horizon re-fit, which yields $N \propto C^{0.5}, D \propto C^{0.5}$ (the famous $D \approx 20N$). We walk Kaplan's bug (cosine-LR decayed to a fixed token horizon biased short-data runs upward), Chinchilla's three-approach triangulation (training-curves / IsoFLOP / parametric-fit, all agreeing within bootstrap), the headline 70B-vs-280B result at matched FLOPs, and the post-Chinchilla industry move to over-train (Llama-3 8B at $D/N \approx 1875$) for inference economics. Then μP/μTransfer as the HP-transfer technology that makes frontier-scale tuning tractable: Table 3, the $1/d$-vs-$1/\sqrt{d}$ attention scaling, what transfers (master LR, Adam betas, schedule shape) and what doesn't (regularization, init std across depth).

### §4 — Optimization (8 pages, anchor section)

**Optimizer choice is one of the largest remaining levers on training compute.** AdamW is the production default (with the decoupled-weight-decay and update-RMS in the 0.2–0.4 range that Muon's paper measures). Lion (sign-momentum) and Sophia (diagonal-Hessian preconditioning) had headline-2× claims that didn't hold on downstream generalization for LLMs. Muon (Newton-Schulz orthogonalization of momentum on matrix params, Liu et al. / Moonlight 2025) is the only published optimizer with ~2× compute-efficiency lift at LLM scale, fitted as $L_{\mathrm{Muon}} = 2.506 \, C^{-0.052}$ vs $L_{\mathrm{AdamW}} = 2.608 \, C^{-0.054}$, demonstrated at 16B-MoE-active-3B / 5.7T tokens (the Moonlight model). We transcribe AdamW Eq. (2), Muon Eq. (3-4) including the Newton-Schulz coefficients $(a,b,c) = (3.4445, -4.7750, 2.0315)$, walk Muon's two scale-stable ingredients (mandatory weight decay, $\sqrt{\max(A,B)} \cdot 0.2$ per-shape RMS scaling that lets AdamW's tuned $\eta, \lambda$ be reused). Schedule axis: cosine-decay (GPT-3, Llama-1/2/3) → WSD (Warmup-Stable-Decay; MiniCPM 2024 origin, theoretical basis arXiv:2410.05192, used by Moonlight in explicit form: 33B-warmup → 5.2T-stable cosine-decay → 500B-cooldown linear decay with high-quality data injection). Schedule-Free (Defazio 2024) as the open frontier.

- Anchors: `kb/notes/training/optimization.md`. `kb/excerpts/muon-moonlight2025.md` (#sec-2-1-update-rule, #sec-2-2-rms, #sec-2-2-wd, #sec-3-2-scaling, #sec-3-3-moonlight).
- Math to transcribe: AdamW Eq. (2) with decoupled $\lambda \theta$; Muon Eq. (3) update with $\mathbf{O}_t = \text{Newton-Schulz}(\mathbf{M}_t)$; Eq. (4) Newton-Schulz iteration with the three coefficients; Muon's empirical scaling fit; WSD's three-phase $\eta_t$ schedule from Moonlight §3.3.
- Tensor-shape table: $\theta \in \mathbb{R}^P$, $\mathbf{W} \in \mathbb{R}^{A \times B}$ for matrix params, $\mathbf{O}_t \in \mathbb{R}^{A \times B}$ as the polar-factor approximation; per-shape $\sqrt{\max(A,B)}$ scaling.
- Contradiction `[CONTRADICTION]`: Lion's reported 1.5–2× speedup vs AdamW catching up on downstream evals (the "training loss vs downstream" gap, also true for Sophia). Treat optimizer speedups as training-loss-conditional until downstream is verified.

### §5 — Distributed training (10 pages, anchor section)

**Distributed training is now five composable axes on a single DeviceMesh, not one parallelism choice.** DP (FSDP / ZeRO-1/2/3 / FSDP2-DTensor), TP+SP (column/row weight shards plus sequence-parallel norm), CP (token-position split via ring-attention), PP (1F1B → ZeroBubble → DualPipe), EP (per-expert all-to-all dispatch + combine). Per-axis cost analysis: per-step communication, per-layer communication, per-microbatch activation, in-flight activations. We walk DeepSeek-V3's plan as case study (ZeRO-1 DP + 16-way PP DualPipe + 64-way EP across 8 nodes + *no TP*; 2.788M H800-hours, $5.576M cluster cost), DualPipe's bidirectional bubble-eater formula $(\frac{p}{2} - 1)(F\&B + B - 3W)$, and the cross-node all-to-all kernel that exploits the 3.2× NVLink-vs-IB bandwidth ratio (token capped at 4 nodes). TorchTitan's 4D composability, Async-TP via SymmetricMemory, FSDP2's per-parameter DTensor sharding (~7% peak-memory reduction). Distributed Muon as the latest add: per-rank Newton-Schulz on partial gradient gathers, 1–3% latency overhead.

- Anchors: `kb/notes/training/distributed-training.md`. `kb/excerpts/{torchtitan2024,deepseek-v3-training,muon-moonlight2025}.md`.
- Math to transcribe: per-axis memory table (params, optim state, activation per-GPU) under DP/ZeRO/TP/PP/CP; bubble formulas 1F1B / ZeroBubble / DualPipe; DeepSeek-V3 token-routing constraint (1 IB transmit per (token, node), then NVLink forward inside node).
- Tensor-shape table: $G = d \cdot t \cdot c \cdot p \cdot e$ on a 5D DeviceMesh; per-GPU state under each axis combination; $A$ peak per-microbatch activation; $P$ parameters; $4P$ for FP32 master + Adam moments; in-flight microbatches under PP scale activations by $p$.
- Contradiction `[CONTRADICTION]`: TP-yes-vs-TP-no — Llama-3 405B used heavy TP+PP+DP, DeepSeek-V3 671B-A37B used *no* TP claiming ZeRO-1 + EP + DualPipe + FP8 had enough activation relief. Real disagreement, not wash; outcome is architecture-conditional.

### §6 — Mixed precision and stability (8 pages)

**The precision axis is independent of the parameter axis, scales the same way, and is now production-stable through FP8.** Three independent precision choices (storage / compute / communication) with the master-FP32-plus-low-precision-working-copy pattern (Micikevicius 2017 Eq. 1). Format zoo (FP32 / FP16 / BF16 / FP8 E4M3+E5M2 / NVFP4 E2M1 / BitNet ternary) with dynamic-range-vs-precision tradeoff. Stability machinery: dynamic loss scaling for FP16, BF16's drop-in-replacement story, DeepSeek-V3's fine-grained FP8 recipe (per-token-per-128-channel activation tile, per-128×128 weight block, all-E4M3, promotion-to-CUDA-cores at $N_C = 128$, sensitive ops kept BF16/FP32, master-and-grads kept FP32). The headline empirical fact: DeepSeek-V3's full 14.8T-token / 2-month / 2048-H800 pre-training reported zero irrecoverable loss spikes. Loss-spike taxonomy (gradient-norm explosion, attention-logit divergence, embedding-norm growth, FP8 underflow on rare tokens, MoE expert collapse) and mitigations (clip, QK-clip / QK-LayerNorm, tied-embedding-norm-scale, kept-precision per op, auxiliary-loss-free load balancing). Optimizer × precision coupling: Muon's update RMS exceeds BF16 high-precision range without weight decay. NVFP4 / BitNet outlook.

### §7 — Supervised fine-tuning (6 pages)

**SFT is plain cross-entropy with prompt-masking — the interesting research questions are in the data, not the loss.** Eq. (1) $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SFT}} = -\mathbb{E} \sum_t m_t \log \pi_\theta(z_t | z_{<t})$ with $m_t = 1$ on response tokens. Lineage from FLAN / FLAN-T5 (instruction-tuning as terminal stage, ~1.8K tasks) → InstructGPT (the 2022 inflection point, ~13K human demonstrations as stage 1 of a multi-stage pipeline) → Llama-3 (rejection-sampling SFT, multi-round, ~10M+ samples) → Tülu-3 (~939K mixed-source) → MAGPIE (self-synthesis from aligned-LM-template-prefix at zero human cost) → R1-Distill (800K samples from RLVR-trained R1, the 2025 result that reasoning capability transfers through pure SFT) → s1 (1K hand-curated reasoning traces + budget-forcing). Multi-turn loss-mask conventions (train-on-all-assistant-turns vs. last-turn-only). Why SFT before RL at all (RL needs a reasonable starting policy; rollouts otherwise unscorable, KL-anchor meaningless).

- Anchors: `kb/notes/post-training/sft.md`. `kb/excerpts/{ouyang2022-instructgpt,phi4,deepseek-r1}.md`.
- Math to transcribe: Eq. (1) SFT loss with prompt mask $m_t$; the forward-KL connection ($\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SFT}}$ ≈ minimizing $\mathrm{KL}(\pi^{\mathrm{demo}} \| \pi_\theta)$ in the infinite-data limit, vs. RLHF's reverse KL).
- Tensor-shape table: $z = (x, y)$ concatenation, mask $m \in \{0,1\}^{|z|}$; epoch counts (1–4 for SFT vs single pass for pre-train); typical LR $5 \times 10^{-6}$ (10× smaller than pre-train peak).

### §8 — RLHF (8 pages, anchor section)

**RLHF is the canonical three-stage pipeline (SFT → reward model → PPO with KL anchor) that converts a base LM into an instruction-following assistant.** InstructGPT Eq. (1) $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{RLHF}} = \mathbb{E}[r_\phi] - \beta \cdot \mathrm{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi^{\mathrm{SFT}})$ with the per-token-in-reward shaping Eq. (5). Bradley-Terry reward-model loss Eq. (3) on $\binom{K}{2}$ pairs per prompt. The closed-form RL optimum Eq. (4) $\pi^*(y|x) \propto \pi^{\mathrm{SFT}}(y|x) \exp(r_\phi/\beta)$, which becomes the foundation for §9's DPO. Stiennon-2020 → InstructGPT-2022 lineage, the 1.3B-RLHF-beats-175B-GPT-3 inflection. The PPO infrastructure problem (4 model copies in memory: policy, reference, reward, value; sampling/scoring/optimization three phases; KL spikes and reward-hacking). PPO-ptx ($\gamma$-weighted pre-training-mixing term) as the alignment-tax mitigation. Reward-model variants: ensembles (Coste 2023), WARM/WARP (Ramé 2024 / Gemma 3, weight-averaged RMs as a robustness mechanism). Why DPO replaced PPO at Llama-3 scale.

- Anchors: `kb/notes/post-training/rlhf.md`. `kb/excerpts/{ouyang2022-instructgpt,rafailov2023-dpo,bai2022-cai,sharma2023-sycophancy}.md`.
- Math to transcribe: Eq. (1) KL-constrained reward maximization; Eq. (2) PPO-ptx term; Eq. (3) Bradley-Terry RM loss; Eq. (4) closed-form RL optimum; Eq. (5) per-token reward shaping with KL anchor.
- Tensor-shape table: 4 model copies (policy / ref / reward / value) × $P$ params; PPO clip range $\epsilon$, KL coefficient $\beta \in [0.01, 0.05]$ in InstructGPT-era; LR $\sim 10^{-6}$.

### §9 — DPO and offline preference (8 pages, anchor section)

**DPO is the closed-form, classification-loss reformulation of the RLHF objective that bypasses RL altogether — same fixed point, dramatically cheaper per step.** Eq. (1) $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{DPO}} = -\mathbb{E} \log \sigma(\beta \log \pi_\theta(y_w|x)/\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}(y_w|x) - \beta \log \pi_\theta(y_l|x)/\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}(y_l|x))$. The reparameterization derivation (invert §8 Eq. (4) to get reward as $r(x,y) = \beta \log \pi^*(y|x)/\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}(y|x) + \beta \log Z(x)$, BT preference cancels $\log Z(x)$, yields preference probability that depends only on policy log-ratios). The implicit-reward structural fact: DPO and PPO-RLHF share the same fixed point on the same preference data — DPO reaches it without sampling, without an RM, without an RL loop. ~2 forward + 1 backward per step vs. PPO's 4-forward + RM-forward + sampling. Variant family (the table is load-bearing): IPO (squared loss on the gap, fixes overconfidence trap), KTO (Kahneman-Tversky on single-rating data), ORPO (no reference, log-odds + SFT), SimPO (reference-free, length-normalized, target-margin $\gamma$), CPO. Anchored-vs-reference-free as the organizing axis. On-policy preference iteration with off-policy optimization (Llama-3, Tülu-3). DPO length-bias and SimPO's $\beta/|y|$ length-normalization fix.

- Anchors: `kb/notes/post-training/dpo-and-offline.md`. `kb/excerpts/{rafailov2023-dpo,meng2024-simpo,ouyang2022-instructgpt}.md`.
- Math to transcribe: DPO Eq. (1) loss, Eq. (2) implicit reward $\hat{r}_\theta = \beta \log \pi_\theta/\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}$, Eq. (4) closed-form RL optimum, the gradient form $\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{DPO}}$; IPO's squared-gap loss; KTO's prospect-theory utility; ORPO's $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SFT}} + \lambda \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{OR}}$; SimPO Eq. ### §10 — RLAIF and Constitutional AI (6 pages)

**CAI is "InstructGPT with a different labeler": same RLHF machinery, AI-judge-under-a-constitution replaces human preferences.** Two-stage pipeline: SL-CAI (sample → critique-under-randomly-chosen-principle → revise → SFT on revisions) → RL-CAI (sample-pair → AI-judge under principle → BT-RM trained on AI labels → PPO with KL anchor to $\pi^{\mathrm{SL-CAI}}$). The constitution as principle prior: ~16 natural-language rules, sampled per-pair so different examples scored under different principles. Behavioral signature: non-evasion (engages with harmful prompts by stating objections rather than refusing flatly). The RLAIF / CAI separation: RLAIF replaces preference source; CAI is RLAIF + explicit constitution. Compose with DPO (AI-preferences feeding DPO directly, no PPO loop). C3AI (2025): which principles transfer. R-CAI (2026): inverted constitution as adversarial / red-team data generator — the dual-use surface. Recursive RLAIF / self-reward as the open frontier with mode-collapse risk.

- Anchors: `kb/notes/post-training/rlaif-and-constitutional.md`. `kb/excerpts/bai2022-cai.md` (#sec-3 SL-CAI, #sec-4 RL-CAI, #sec-5 CoT enhancement, #sec-6 behavioral).

### §11 — RLVR and GRPO (8 pages, anchor section)

**RLVR replaces the learned reward model with a programmatic verifier; GRPO replaces the value model with a group-mean baseline.** The verifiable-reward setting: $R_{\mathrm{verifier}}(x, y) = \mathbb{1}[\mathrm{verify}(x, y) = \mathsf{true}] \in \{0, 1\}$, sparse and terminal. Sidesteps reward-hacking (the only way to score 1 is to actually solve the problem) and reward-overoptimization (verifier *is* the gold reward, modulo wrong gold answers). GRPO objective Eq. (1) with importance ratio $r_{i,t}$, group-normalized advantage Eq. (2) $\tilde{A}_i = (R_i - \mathrm{mean})/\mathrm{std}$ constant in $t$, KL anchor $\beta \mathrm{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi_{\mathrm{ref}})$ inside the objective. The memory profile that makes GRPO matter at LLM scale: PPO+GAE requires a value model co-sized with the policy (doubles active parameters and activation memory); GRPO's group-mean baseline removes it. The DeepSeek-R1 reward set: accuracy reward (binary, sympy-equivalence / unit-tests / multi-choice exact-match) + small format reward for `<think>...</think>` block presence. R1's headline trajectory (R1-Zero from 15.6% → 77.9% on AIME 2024 over GRPO training; "aha-moment" `wait`-token spike). DAPO's four targeted improvements (Clip-Higher asymmetric, Dynamic Sampling rejecting all-equal-$R$ groups, Token-Level Loss equalizing across trajectory length, Overlong Reward Shaping). VAPO's careful-value-model variant, VinePPO's MC re-simulation, REINFORCE++ family. Cross-ref Paper 3 for inference-time use of RLVR-trained reasoners and the deeper search-and-verification triangle.

### §12 — Adaptation and merging (6 pages)

**Adapt and merge are weight-space operations, not data-space — the unit of action is parameter-level.** Two load-bearing scientific topics: PEFT (LoRA, QLoRA) and merging (Soups, Task Vectors, TIES). LoRA Eq. (1) $W = W_0 + \alpha BA$ with rank $r$ low-rank update, $A$-init $\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$ and $B$-init zero (so $\Delta W = 0$ at start, model behaves identically). Trainable params $r(d_{\mathrm{out}} + d_{\mathrm{in}})$ vs full $d_{\mathrm{out}} \cdot d_{\mathrm{in}}$, typically $10^4 \times$ fewer. Merge for inference: $W = W_0 + \alpha BA$ once, no latency overhead. QLoRA: 4-bit NF4 quantization of $W_0$, double-quantized scales, paged optimizers — 65B fine-tune fits in 48GB GPU. Task vector $\tau_T = \theta_f - \theta_0$. Model Soup uniform/greedy averaging Eq. (4). TIES-Merging trim → elect-sign → disjoint-merge to resolve sign-conflict interference; DARE drop-and-rescale; SLERP for two-model merges; evolutionary CMA-ES merge-search (Sakana 2024). MergeKit as the canonical OSS toolkit. Continual / late-stage pre-training (Phi-4 midtrain, OLMo-2 Dolmino, DeepSeek-V3 context-extension) as the data-side mechanism that pairs with WSD-cooldown LR schedules from §4. The linear-mode-connectivity story as the speculative theoretical underpinning.

- Anchors: `kb/notes/training/adaptation-and-merging.md`. `kb/excerpts/{hu2021-lora,dettmers2023-qlora,wortsman2022-model-soups,ilharco2022-task-vectors,yadav2023-ties-merging,phi4,deepseek-v3-training}.md`.
- Math to transcribe: LoRA Eq. (1), QLoRA Eq. (2) $y = \mathrm{dequant}(W_0^{\mathrm{NF4}}) x + \alpha B(Ax)$; task-vector Eq. (3) $\tau_T = \theta_f - \theta_0$; uniform-soup Eq. (4) $\theta_{\mathrm{soup}} = \frac{1}{K} \sum_k \theta_k$; TIES three-step procedure (trim top-$k$%, elect $s_j = \mathrm{sign}(\sum_i \tau_{i,j})$, disjoint-merge over $\mathcal{A}_j$).
- Tensor-shape table: $r \in \{8, 16, 32, 64\}$, $\alpha = 2r$ typical; NF4 4-bit + 8-bit double-quantized scales = ~0.37 bits/param overhead; merge-method comparison table (sign / sparsity / multi-task quality).
- Contradiction `[CONTRADICTION]`: LoRA-quality-at-scale — academic view ($r=8$ recovers full-FT on standard benchmarks) vs production view (LoRA underperforms full-FT on reasoning-heavy / long-context at frontier; need $r = 64$–$128$). Task-distribution-dependent.

### §14 — Contradictions live and open frontiers (4 pages)

Concentrated discussion of `[CONTRADICTION]` markers from §2-§12. Each gets explicit treatment — what each side claims, what evidence would close it.

1. **Kaplan-vs-Chinchilla compute allocation.** Methodologically resolved (Kaplan's LR-horizon bug); what remains is the "Chinchilla trap" framing — frontier industry over-trains past Chinchilla for inference economics, which is *not* a refutation but a different objective. Open: Chinchilla-conditional-on-quality, MoE / hybrid-arch exponents, multilingual / code-heavy mix shifts.
2. **Optimizer speedups are training-loss-conditional.** Lion's 1.5–2× and Sophia's 2× claims didn't hold on downstream evals. Muon-Moonlight's ~2× compute-efficiency lift is the strongest current candidate, demonstrated at 16B-MoE / 5.7T but not at 70B+ dense or 600B+ MoE.
3. **TP-yes-vs-TP-no.** Llama-3-405B (heavy TP+PP+DP) vs DeepSeek-V3 (no TP, ZeRO-1+EP+DualPipe+FP8). Architecture-conditional; not a universal answer.
4. **FP8 / NVFP4 / BitNet production status.** FP8 is production-stable post-DeepSeek-V3 (zero-spike 14.8T-token result). NVFP4 is shipping in Blackwell hardware but no major lab has publicly confirmed FP4 pre-training. BitNet 1.58 ternary's 70B claims have thin independent reproduction.
5. **SFT data quantity-vs-quality.** LIMA / s1 (1K-10K curated suffice) vs Tülu-3 / Llama-3 (1M+ for capability breadth). Both true; not yet reconciled regime-by-regime.
6. **Reward-hacking is fundamental or tractable.** Tractable-view (WARM/WARP, Gemma 3 production) vs. fundamental-view (arXiv:2406.02900 scaling-laws-for-direct-alignment, sycophancy as persistent shortcut). Open.
7. **DPO-vs-PPO at frontier scale.** Llama-3 / Tülu-3 view (DPO better) vs. DeepMind / Gemma-3 view (PPO+WARM/WARP still in production). No controlled head-to-head.
8. **RLAIF / CAI: internalization or surface mimicry.** Behaviorally indistinguishable on standard evals; only adversarial probes separate. Open. CAI-vs-sycophancy: judge rewards principle-shaped text whether reasoning is shallow or deep.
9. **Does RLVR create new reasoning capability?** DeepSeek-R1 framing vs Yue et al. NeurIPS 2025 oral (RLVR is sampling-efficient sharpening, not capability expansion). 2026-05 consensus leans toward Yue with caveats; cross-ref Paper 3.
10. **Model-merging guarantees.** Linear-mode-connectivity is the speculative basin-sharing hypothesis; "merged > best ingredient" is task-set-conditional. No canonical theory predicts when merging works vs. produces interference.
11. **Precision × token-count interaction.** Kumar 2024's effective-parameter prediction (training data $D$ growth makes models harder to quantize past a threshold) vs. industry over-training for inference economics. The trade-off between Llama-3-style $D/N \approx 1875$ and post-quantization cost is incompletely mapped.
12. **μP at frontier scale.** Strict Definition 4.1 vs "MetaP-like" loose variants; independent positive results outside Microsoft / Cerebras / Graphcore are scarce. Frontier labs disclose unevenly.

Closing thesis: the pipeline's open questions are *localized* — most live at stage boundaries (precision × optimizer; token-count × quantization; preference × verifier) rather than at the pipeline's structure. The structure (pre-train → scale → optimize → distribute → precision → SFT → preference → reasoning → merge) is now stable; the empirical envelope around each stage's variables is what 2027-class results will close.
