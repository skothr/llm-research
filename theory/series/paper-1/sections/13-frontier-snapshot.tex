\section{Frontier-2026 snapshot: what choices the leading models share}
\label{sec:frontier-snapshot}

The previous ten sections walked the architectural axes one at a
time. This section composes them. We tabulate the per-axis choices
made by the 2026 frontier model lineup --- DeepSeek V3 and R1, Llama
4, Mistral Large 2, Gemma 3, Qwen 2.5 / Qwen 3, plus the partially
disclosed Claude 3.7-class, GPT-class, and Gemini 2.5 systems --- on
the same eight axes used throughout this paper, and call out where
the published architectures agree and where they diverge.
\textbf{The thesis of this section: the convergence is real, narrow,
and concentrated in five of the eight axes; the remaining three
(KV-sharing strategy, dense-vs-MoE on the FFN, and the multimodal
integration pattern) are the live splits, and only one of them
(KV-sharing) cleanly partitions the disclosed lineup.}

The table that follows replaces the model-zoo prose of vendor blog
posts with a uniform ten-column row per model. Where a lab has
published a primary source we cite it; where a detail is vendor-blog
or release-card disclosure only we mark
\deepencite{citation-pending}; where the architecture is undisclosed
we record ``not disclosed'' rather than guess from inference-stack
reverse engineering, which is unreliable across point releases.

\subsection{Disclosed cluster: the open-weights frontier}
\label{sec:snapshot-disclosed}

The seven rows in the upper block of \cref{tab:frontier-snapshot} are
either fully open-weights with an architectural tech report, or
disclose architectural detail in a public release card. We walk them
briefly to anchor each cell.

\paragraph{DeepSeek V3 and R1.}
DeepSeek V3 is a 671B-total / 37B-active MoE with
$E = 256$, $E_s = 1$, $k = 8$, auxiliary-loss-free
balancing~\citep[\S 2.2]{deepseek-v3}.\footnote{KB excerpt:
\texttt{kb/excerpts/deepseek-v3.md\#sec-2}.} The attention sublayer
uses MLA with joint-compression rank $d_c = 512$ and the
decoupled-RoPE construction of \citet[\S 2.1.2 Eq.~9]{deepseek-v2}.
Multi-Token Prediction with one auxiliary head is part of the
pretraining objective~\citep[\S 2.3.1]{deepseek-v3}.
RMSNorm in Pre-LN placement is inherited from the LLaMA
template~\citep{touvron2023}. DeepSeek-R1 is a continued-RL fine-tune
of V3-base with no architectural changes other than a training-stage
thinking-mode toggle~\citep[\S 2]{deepseek-r1}; its row repeats V3's
choices.

\paragraph{Llama 4 (Scout, Maverick).}
LLaMA 4 ships two MoE configurations: Scout with $E = 16$ and
Maverick with $E = 128$, both with $E_s = 1$, $k = 1$, and the FFN
sublayer alternated between dense SwiGLU and MoE per
layer~\citep[\S 2.1]{meta-llama4}.\deepencite{meta-llama4 §2.1 ---
Llama 4 release card / Meta release blog, citation-pending: the
\texttt{meta-llama4} cite-key currently maps only the long-context /
iRoPE detail; the MoE and native-multimodal architectural detail
needs consolidation into a separate \texttt{meta-llama4-release}
entry.} Position encoding is iRoPE (3:1
RoPE-to-NoPE) with temperature scaling on the NoPE
layers~\citep[\S 2.1, \S 2.4]{meta-llama4}, trained natively at
$256\text{K}$. KV-sharing is GQA-8, inherited from
LLaMA-3~\citep[\S 3.1]{meta-llama3}. Multimodality is native: image
and text are jointly pretrained from scratch~\citep[\S 2]{meta-llama4}.

\paragraph{Mistral Large 2.}
Mistral Large 2 is dense SwiGLU throughout, with GQA-8 attention and
RMSNorm + Pre-LN inherited from the Mistral 7B
template~\citep[\S 2]{jiang2023}.\deepencite{citation-pending: Mistral
Large 2 release notes / Hugging Face model card, July 2024;
\texttt{mistral-large-2} is not yet a references.bib entry.}
Position encoding is RoPE; multi-token output is not disclosed beyond
single-head autoregression. The Pixtral variant adds a projector +
vision encoder (\cref{sec:multimodal}); the text-only Mistral Large
2 is not multimodal.\footnote{\textbf{Superseded 2025-12-02.}
Mistral Large 3 \citep{mistral-large3-2025} crosses the
Mistral flagship line over to MoE: 675B-total / 41B-active sparse
MoE under Apache 2.0, 256K context, native multimodal. The
\texttt{Mistral Large 2} row is retained here because its detail is
the publicly-documented dense reference point Paper 1 \S\ref{sec:ffn}
uses as a counter-example; on the dense-vs-MoE roster of
\S\ref{sec:snapshot-divergence}, the Mistral row should now be read
as MoE going forward, and the dense-vs-MoE split tilts further
toward MoE than the row alone shows.}

\paragraph{Gemma 3.}
Gemma 3 27B uses GQA with the interleaved local / global attention
pattern of Gemma 2~\citep[\S 2.1]{gemma3} --- alternating per layer
between sliding-window local and full global attention. Normalization
is the Peri-LN placement of \citet{peri-ln2025} combined with QK-Norm
on the attention path~\citep[\S 2.1]{gemma3,olmo2}. Position encoding
is RoPE; the FFN is dense SwiGLU; multimodality is native via a
SigLIP-class vision
encoder.\deepencite{gemma3 §3: multimodal pipeline detail;
verify SigLIP variant against the Gemma 3 release card.}

\paragraph{Qwen 2.5 and Qwen 3.}
Qwen 2.5 is dense SwiGLU with GQA, RMSNorm + Pre-LN, RoPE + YaRN, and
a $152$K-vocab BPE tokenizer.\deepencite{citation-pending: Qwen 2.5
release card; \texttt{qwen3} maps the Qwen 3 paper, not Qwen 2.5
architectural detail.} Qwen 3 (235B-A22B) replaces the dense FFN with
a fine-grained MoE at $E = 128$, $E_s = 0$, $k = 8$, with
global-batch load balancing in place of auxiliary-loss
balancing~\citep[\S 2.1]{qwen3}. Crucially Qwen 3 keeps GQA rather
than MLA, despite training in the same year as DeepSeek V3 --- the
lab-level KV-sharing split flagged in
\cref{sec:attention-kv-sharing}. The multimodal line (Qwen 2.5-VL)
uses a projector, not native multimodal.

\subsection{Undisclosed cluster: Claude, GPT, Gemini}
\label{sec:snapshot-undisclosed}

The lower block of \cref{tab:frontier-snapshot} is the closed-source
frontier. None of Anthropic (Claude 3.5 / 3.7 / 4-class), OpenAI
(GPT-4o, o1 / o3, GPT-5), or Google DeepMind (Gemini 2.5) has
published per-axis architectural detail at the level of the open
labs. The model cards report capabilities, training data scale,
context length, and multimodal coverage; they do not report
KV-sharing strategy, attention-implementation kernel, FFN family, or
normalization placement.\deepencite{Anthropic Claude 3.7 system card
/ OpenAI GPT-4o and GPT-5 system cards / Google Gemini 2.5
technical report --- citation-pending: vendor system cards are
discovery-only sources (Tier B/C in
\texttt{theory/sources/README.md}) and cannot solely back hard
architectural claims; consolidate into separate cite-keys once
formal venues catch up.} What is publicly known is restricted to:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Tokenization family.} OpenAI's tiktoken family is
publicly documented; Anthropic's BPE-class tokenizer is documented
in the API reference but not in a primary technical report; Gemini
uses a SentencePiece-class tokenizer disclosed in the Gemma 3 paper
\citep[\S 2.3]{gemma3} as a sibling to Gemini's own.
\item \emph{Multimodal pattern.} GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet onward,
and Gemini 2.5 are native-multimodal (image + audio + text in a
single pretraining); this is disclosed at capability level in each
vendor's system card and is the third integration pattern of
\cref{sec:multimodal}.
\item \emph{Reasoning-mode toggle.} OpenAI o1 / o3 and (since 3.7)
Claude expose a thinking-mode generation phase, mirroring the
DeepSeek-R1 / Qwen 3 toggle~\citep[\S 2]{deepseek-r1, qwen3}.
Whether this requires architectural support beyond training-stage
changes is contested
(\cref{sec:state-space-and-hybrids,sec:multi-token-output}) and
not resolved by the closed-vendor disclosures.
\end{itemize}

\begin{forumsignal}
Inference-stack reverse-engineering of closed-vendor models (latency
patterns; per-token cost differentials; rough head-count estimation
from KV-cache utilization scaling) circulates in tier-C community
sources but is unreliable across point releases and unverifiable
without vendor cooperation. We treat all three undisclosed cells as
``ND'' rather than risk laundering forum speculation as architectural
fact. The convergence claim of this section is therefore restricted
to the disclosed seven rows; whether the closed labs sit inside or
outside the disclosed convergence cluster is a falsifiable prediction
of \cref{sec:contradictions}, not a claim of this section.
\end{forumsignal}

\paragraph{Caveat: closed-vendor row labels age faster than the
architectural picture.} The closed-row labels in
\cref{tab:frontier-snapshot} (``Claude 3.7-class,'' ``GPT-class
(o-/$\!4o$/5),'' ``Gemini 2.5'') are accurate as a 2025-Q1 vintage of
the closed lineup. By 2026-Q2 each lab has shipped multiple point
releases beyond those labels --- Anthropic's Claude 4-family
(May 2025) plus subsequent Opus 4.x and Sonnet 4.x updates; OpenAI's
GPT-5 (August 2025) folding the o-series into a unified
fast-vs-thinking tier, with subsequent GPT-5.x point releases; and
Gemini 2.5 Deep Think (August 2025) with its parallel-thinking RL
disclosure. The architectural picture this section's thesis depends on
\emph{does not move} across those point releases at any disclosed
level: the closed rows remain ``ND'' on every architectural axis the
table tracks, and the productionised exposure of inference-time
compute (extended-thinking budgets, reasoning-effort levels,
parallel-thinking modes) converges on the same API surface as the
disclosed labs rather than diverging from it.\deepencite{closed-vendor system
cards (Anthropic Claude 4-family, OpenAI GPT-5.x, Google Gemini 2.5
Deep Think) are tier-B per \texttt{theory/sources/README.md} and back
the version-lineage / API-surface claims, not the architectural cells;
those cells remain ``ND.''} We retain the 2025-Q1 row labels with
this date stamp rather than extend them per release: the column-by-
column architectural cells would still be ``ND'' for every newer
label, and the row's load-bearing function in the table is the
disclosure-ceiling, not the version stamp. The convergence claim of
this section is therefore stable across the closed-row lineage shift
between 2025-Q1 and 2026-Q2; the lineage shift itself is documented
in \cref{sec:contradictions} as a separate question about whether the
disclosure ceiling is moving.

\subsection{Where the disclosed frontier agrees}
\label{sec:snapshot-agreement}

Five of the eight axes show near-universal agreement across the
seven disclosed rows of \cref{tab:frontier-snapshot}.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Normalization: RMSNorm + Pre-LN-class placement.} All
seven disclosed models use RMSNorm with the norm placed before each
sublayer rather than after the residual add. Gemma 3 and OLMo 2
extend this to Peri-LN with QK-Norm~\citep{gemma3,olmo2}; the base
choice (RMSNorm + Pre-LN + $1/\sqrt{N}$ residual init) is universal
(\cref{sec:normalization-axis}).

\item \textbf{Positional encoding: RoPE plus a long-context
extension.} All seven disclosed rows apply RoPE to $Q, K$ at every
attention layer (\cref{sec:positional-encoding}). The extension varies ---
vanilla, YaRN, iRoPE, decoupled-RoPE for MLA, interleaved
local / global --- but the base mechanism (multiplicative rotation
of $Q, K$ parameterised by $\theta_i = \theta^{-2(i-1)/d_h}$) is
shared.

\item \textbf{FFN gating: SwiGLU.} Every disclosed model uses
SwiGLU as the per-position FFN nonlinearity, whether the sublayer
is dense or MoE. The 3-matrix
$\mathrm{FFN}(x) = (W_3 (\mathrm{Swish}(W_1 x) \odot W_2 x))$
form of \citet[Eq.~6]{shazeer2020} is universal; the dense-vs-MoE
split is on the multiplicity around it, not on the activation.

\item \textbf{KV-sharing: head-count reduction.} No disclosed
2026-frontier model uses full MHA. Every row sits at GQA-8,
GQA-with-windowing, or MLA --- all reducing $H_{\mathrm{kv}}$ below
$H_q$. The lab-aligned split between GQA and MLA persists, but
\emph{some} form of head-count reduction is universal, and every
major inference framework (FlashAttention, vLLM, SGLang) now ships
first-class GQA and increasingly first-class
MLA~\citep{kwon2023,dao2023}.

\item \textbf{Attention implementation: FlashAttention-family exact
attention.} Every disclosed model uses a FlashAttention-class
exact-attention kernel: FA2 on Ampere, FA3 on Hopper, with
MLA-aware variants for the DeepSeek
stack~\citep{dao2023,shah2024,deepseek-v3}. NSA's three-branch
sparse attention~\citep{yuan2025} has not yet shipped at production
scale at any disclosed lab; the formula
$\softmax(QK^\top/\sqrt{d_h})V$ is unchanged across implementations.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{intuition}
Five-of-eight convergence is striking but consistent with the rest
of the paper: the axes that have converged are the ones where the
\emph{primary citation set} of \cref{sec:positional-encoding} through
\cref{sec:normalization-axis} contains a single dominant lineage
(RoPE, SwiGLU, RMSNorm + Pre-LN, head-count-reduced KV-sharing,
FlashAttention-class implementation). The axes that remain split
are the ones where the primary set still contains parallel
lineages (MoE-vs-dense; cross-attention-vs-projector-vs-native
multimodal; GQA-vs-MLA at the lab level). Returning to canonical
math: the residual-stream forward pass on a 2026 frontier model
collapses to the same five-line equation (RMSNorm $\to$ rotated-$Q,K$
attention with reduced $H_{\mathrm{kv}}$ $\to$ residual-add $\to$
RMSNorm $\to$ SwiGLU FFN or routed SwiGLU MoE $\to$ residual-add)
across every disclosed row. The convergence is not a stylistic
preference; it is a fixed point of the per-axis arguments in
\cref{sec:tokenization-embedding,sec:positional-encoding,sec:attention-kv-sharing,sec:attention-hardware-axis,sec:ffn,sec:normalization-axis}.
\end{intuition}

\subsection{Where the disclosed frontier diverges}
\label{sec:snapshot-divergence}

Three axes carry live splits in \cref{tab:frontier-snapshot}.

\paragraph{KV-sharing strategy: GQA versus MLA.}
The split is lab-aligned, not capability-aligned. DeepSeek V2 / V3 /
R1 use MLA throughout~\citep{deepseek-v2,deepseek-v3,deepseek-r1};
every other disclosed lab (Meta, Mistral, Google, Alibaba) uses GQA.
The contradiction in \cref{sec:attention-kv-sharing} is that MLA's
quality story --- matched MHA quality at $4\times$ smaller KV-cache
--- is a single-source primary claim
(\citealp{deepseek-v2}) and has not been independently reproduced
at frontier scale by a non-DeepSeek lab. The serving-stack maturity
gap (FlashAttention's GQA path being older than its MLA path) and
the recipe risk of MLA's decoupled-RoPE construction
(\cref{sec:attention-kv-sharing}) are plausible explanations for the
non-crossover. \cref{sec:contradictions} returns to this.

\paragraph{Dense versus MoE on the FFN.}
The dense-versus-MoE split is genuine but tilting further toward MoE
through 2025--26. LLaMA-3 8B and 70B are dense~\citep{meta-llama3};
Qwen 2.5 base is dense; Mistral Large 2 was dense, but the lab's
flagship line crossed to MoE with Mistral Large 3 (Dec 2025;
\citealp{mistral-large3-2025}: 675B-total / 41B-active sparse MoE).
DeepSeek V3 is MoE with $E = 256$, $E_s = 1$, $k = 8$; Qwen 3 is MoE
with $E = 128$, $E_s = 0$, $k = 8$; Llama 4 is hybrid (alternating
dense and MoE layers); Mixtral 8$\times$7B remains the canonical
8-expert classical MoE point. As discussed in
\cref{sec:ffn-frontier}, this is no longer a question of whether
to do MoE at scale --- the answer is yes for the largest models in
this list --- but of which MoE design point: $E$, $E_s$, $k$, and
load-balancing discipline. The contradiction in
\cref{sec:ffn} is the matched-active-parameter quality
claim~\citep{deepseek-v3,deepseekmoe2024}: that fine-grained MoE
matches dense quality at matched active parameters. Cross-lab
reproduction at frontier scale is sparse, and Qwen 3's
shared-expert-free choice~\citep{qwen3} explicitly disagrees with
DeepSeek V3 on whether $E_s > 0$ helps.

\paragraph{Multimodal integration pattern.}
Three patterns coexist in \cref{tab:frontier-snapshot}: native
multimodal pretraining (LLaMA 4, Gemma 3 multimodal variant,
Claude 3.5+, GPT-4o / 5, Gemini 2.5), projector + LLM
(Qwen 2.5-VL, Pixtral / Mistral Pixtral), and the
cross-attention-bridge variant (no current frontier model; Flamingo
descendants are off-frontier). The cross-attention bridge of
\citet{alayrac2022-flamingo} has effectively been replaced by the
two simpler patterns; the live split is between projector and
native multimodal. The vendor claim that native multimodal produces
better quality at smaller active parameter count
(\cref{sec:multimodal}) is a forum signal pending controlled
cross-lab reproduction, and the closed-vendor cluster's coverage
here is uniform but undisclosed at architectural detail.

\subsection{Closing: which axes have converged, which remain mixed}
\label{sec:snapshot-closing}

The empirical state of the disclosed 2026 frontier is summarized in
two sentences. \textbf{The convergence is a five-axis fixed point
across all disclosed labs}: RoPE-with-extension, RMSNorm in a
Pre-LN-class placement, SwiGLU per-position FFN, head-count-reduced
KV-sharing (some form of GQA or MLA), FlashAttention-class exact
attention; \textbf{the residual splits are concentrated in three
axes}: KV-sharing strategy (GQA vs MLA, lab-aligned, single-source
quality claim), dense-vs-MoE on the FFN multiplicity (genuine
quality / serving-cost trade with cross-lab disagreement on
$E_s > 0$), and multimodal integration pattern (projector vs native,
no controlled cross-lab study). The undisclosed cluster (Claude,
GPT, Gemini) is silent on all eight axes at architectural detail
and provides no signal on whether it sits inside or outside the
disclosed convergence neighbourhood.

The remaining contradictions surfaced through
\cref{sec:positional-encoding,sec:attention-kv-sharing,sec:attention-hardware-axis,sec:ffn,sec:state-space-and-hybrids,sec:multi-token-output,sec:multimodal,sec:inference-adjuncts}
--- MLA-vs-MHA at frontier scale, NSA-vs-FA3 head-to-head,
SSM-hybrid quality at frontier, the matched-active MoE quality gap,
long-context extrapolation method comparison, MTP at non-DeepSeek
scale, native-multimodal quality lift --- are now visible as a
single concentrated set. \Cref{sec:contradictions} treats them as
the open frontier of the architecture story, and asks for each what
evidence would close the gap. The five-axis convergence of this
section is the substrate against which those open questions are
posed; without naming the convergence, the contradictions cannot be
localized; without localizing the contradictions, the field's
remaining architectural questions cannot be made falsifiable.
