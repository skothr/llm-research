\title{
  The modern Transformer is a small set of choices: \\
  convergent architecture in frontier LLMs, 2017--2026
}
\author{Theory KB --- Paper 1 of 5}
\date{2026-05-04 (draft)}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We argue that frontier LLMs in 2026 are converging on a small,
identifiable set of architectural choices along a few orthogonal axes:
KV-sharing (MHA $\to$ MQA $\to$ GQA $\to$ MLA), attention implementation
(FA1 $\to$ FA2 $\to$ FA3, NSA), feed-forward style (dense $\to$
fine-grained MoE with shared experts), normalization (LN $\to$ RMSNorm,
Post-LN $\to$ Pre-LN with $1/\sqrt{N}$ residual init), positional
encoding (sinusoidal $\to$ RoPE/YaRN), tokenization (BPE $\to$
SentencePiece byte-level), and multi-token output (speculative decoding
$\to$ MTP). For each axis we formalize the canonical 2017 baseline,
trace the choices made between 2017 and 2026, and identify the localized
open frontiers --- MLA-vs-MHA at scale, NSA-vs-FA3, SSM-hybrids at
frontier --- where the convergence is still contested.
\end{abstract}
