\section{Self-consistency: sampling and majority vote}
\label{sec:self-consistency}

\citet{wang2022-self-consistency} replace the greedy decoder of
\cref{sec:cot-phenomenon} with the cheapest non-trivial inference-time
compute strategy in the reasoning literature: sample $N$ \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{chain-of-thought}
traces independently at temperature $T > 0$, extract the final answer
from each, and return the one that appears most often. No verifier is
trained, no search tree is expanded, no model parameter is changed.
Despite this minimality, \glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency} lifts greedy \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{CoT} by
\textbf{+17.9\%} on GSM8K, \textbf{+11.0\%} on SVAMP, and \textbf{+12.2\%}
on AQuA \citep[abstract]{wang2022-self-consistency}.\footnote{KB excerpt:
\texttt{kb/excerpts/wang2022-\glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency}.md\#abstract}.} The
result reframes the basic \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{CoT} decoder as one point on a sampling-budget
axis, and supplies the baseline against which every later test-time
compute strategy in this paper — best-of-$N$ with verifier
(\cref{sec:process-reward-models}), tree-search (\cref{sec:tree-search}),
\glsterm{rlvr-3}{RLVR}-trained long traces (\cref{sec:rlvr,sec:r1-family}) — must show
its incremental \glsterm{value}{value}.

\subsection{The mechanism}
\label{sec:sc-mechanism}

Let $x$ be a problem, $z$ a reasoning trace, and $y$ the final answer
extracted from $z$ by a regex on the trailing \textit{``The answer is
$\langle\cdot\rangle$''} pattern \citep[\S 3.1]{wei2022-cot}. The \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{CoT}
decoder of \cref{eq:cot-decode} samples a single trace, $z \sim
p_\theta(\cdot \mid x)$, and answers $y \sim p_\theta(\cdot \mid x, z)$.
\glsterm{self-consistency-2}{Self-consistency} replaces this single draw with $N$ independent draws
and aggregates by plurality
\citep[\S 3]{wang2022-self-consistency}:\footnote{KB excerpt:
\texttt{kb/excerpts/wang2022-\glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency}.md\#sec-3}.}
\begin{equation}
  \hat y \;=\; \argmax_y \; \sum_{n=1}^N \mathbb{1}\bigl[y^{(n)} = y\bigr],
  \qquad
  z^{(n)} \sim p_\theta(\cdot \mid x), \;\;
  y^{(n)} \sim p_\theta(\cdot \mid x, z^{(n)}).
  \label{eq:self-consistency}
\end{equation}
Sampling is at temperature $T > 0$ (top-$k$ or nucleus optional);
the \glsterm{value}{value} $T = 0.7$ used by \citet[\S 4.1]{wang2022-self-consistency}
on GSM8K is a recurring default in the downstream literature.

\begin{lstlisting}[mathescape=true, numbers=none, frame=single, caption={Self-consistency \citep[\S 3]{wang2022-self-consistency}. Inputs: problem $x$, model $p_\theta$, sample count $N$, temperature $T>0$. Output: aggregated answer $\hat y$.}, label=alg:sc]
function self_consistency($x$, $p_\theta$, $N$, $T$):
    $\mathcal{Y} \leftarrow \emptyset$                           # collected answers
    for $n = 1$ to $N$:
        $z^{(n)} \sim p_\theta(\cdot \mid x)$ at temperature $T$ # sample trace
        $y^{(n)} \leftarrow$ extract_answer($z^{(n)}$)           # regex / parser
        $\mathcal{Y} \leftarrow \mathcal{Y} \cup \{y^{(n)}\}$
    return $\arg\max_y \sum_{n=1}^N \mathbb{1}[y^{(n)} = y]$     # majority vote
\end{lstlisting}

The compute cost is $C \propto N$ and the $N$ samples are embarrassingly
parallel, sharing only the prompt-prefix \glsterm{kv-cache}{KV cache}
\citep[\S 2.1]{snell2024}. Tensor shapes: each sample is a sequence of
shape $\mathsf{S}_n \le L_{\max}$ tokens; the answer space the model
votes over is finite and (for the targeted benchmarks) low-cardinality
— integer answers on GSM8K and SVAMP, multiple-choice letters on AQuA
\citep[\S 4]{wang2022-self-consistency}.

\citet[\S 3]{wang2022-self-consistency} present the same mechanism in a
Bayesian framing: $\hat y$ is the mode of the marginal
$p_\theta(y \mid x) = \sum_z p_\theta(y \mid x, z)\, p_\theta(z \mid x)$
estimated by Monte Carlo over the $N$ traces. Marginalising out $z$ is
the conceptual contribution; the unweighted count is the simplest
estimator of the marginal mode. Later work adds importance weights from
verifiers (\cref{sec:process-reward-models}); the weight $w_n = 1$ here
recovers Wang et al.

\subsection{Why it works}
\label{sec:sc-why}

The empirical fact that majority-over-traces beats any single trace
holds whenever two conditions are met. First, the model places higher
probability mass on \emph{correct} reasoning paths than on any single
\emph{incorrect} one — even if no individual sample is more likely
than $0.5$, the correct answer can be the modal answer across samples
when the model spreads error mass across many distinct wrong
trajectories. Second, the answer space is small enough that the
correct-and-correlated samples accumulate on a single bin while errors
diffuse \citep[\S 3]{wang2022-self-consistency}. Both conditions are
empirically satisfied on math word problems and constrained-choice
benchmarks; both fail on open-ended generation
(\cref{sec:sc-when-fails}).

The connection to ensembling theory is direct. Treat each draw as a
noisy classifier $\hat y^{(n)}$ with $\Pr[\hat y^{(n)} = y^*] = p$ for
some $p > 1/|\mathcal{Y}|$; the $N$ draws are exchangeable and weakly
dependent through the shared prompt. The probability that the
plurality of $N$ samples equals $y^*$ approaches $1$ as $N \to \infty$
by the law of large numbers, with rate determined by the gap
$p - \max_{y \ne y^*} \Pr[\hat y^{(n)} = y]$. The rate is
\emph{multiplicative} in this gap, which is why a small per-sample
edge produces a large vote-margin advantage at moderate $N$. This is
the standard variance-reduction argument used to justify ensemble
voting in classical ML, transposed to the language-model decoder
\citep[\S 3]{wang2022-self-consistency}.

Why $T > 0$ is required: \glsterm{greedy-decoding}{greedy decoding} ($T = 0$) returns the same
trace every time, so $N > 1$ samples carry no additional information.
\citet[\S 4.3]{wang2022-self-consistency} ablate temperature and find
gains stable across $T \in [0.4, 0.7]$, with degradation at very high
$T$ (the per-sample correctness probability $p$ drops below the
threshold where majority recovers it) and at $T \to 0$ (samples
collapse to the greedy trajectory).

\begin{intuition}
\glsterm{self-consistency-2}{Self-consistency} is ``ask the model the same question $N$ times and
take the answer it gives most often.'' The folk gloss is exactly right;
the only subtlety is that the question is asked at $T > 0$ so the $N$
answers are not literally identical. Returning to math: $\hat y$ in
\cref{eq:self-consistency} is the mode of the empirical distribution
$\hat p_N(y \mid x) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_n \mathbb{1}[y^{(n)} = y]$, which
is a Monte Carlo estimator of the answer marginal
$p_\theta(y \mid x) = \E_{z \sim p_\theta(\cdot \mid x)}\bigl[
p_\theta(y \mid x, z)\bigr]$ filtered through the deterministic
extraction map $z \mapsto y$. The estimator is unbiased and consistent
in $N$; the rate of convergence is governed by the variance of the
indicator, which shrinks fastest when the answer marginal is peaked on
the correct $y^*$.
\end{intuition}

\subsection{Headline results}
\label{sec:sc-results}

\citet{wang2022-self-consistency} report gains on five benchmarks at the
\glsterm{palm}{PaLM}-540B / GPT-3-175B scale where greedy \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{CoT} is itself
non-trivial \citep[abstract]{wang2022-self-consistency}:

SVAMP  (arithmetic word problems) & $+11.0\%$ \\

\footnotetext{KB excerpt:

The gains are produced by a single change to the decoder: from greedy
to $N$-sample-plus-majority-vote at $T = 0.7$. No new model is trained,
no exemplars are added to the \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{CoT} prompt, no answer verifier is
introduced. \citet[\S 4]{wang2022-self-consistency} additionally report
a \glsterm{saturation}{saturation} curve in $N$: gains are stable across $N \in [10, 40]$
with diminishing returns past $N \approx
40$.\footnote{KB excerpt:
\texttt{kb/excerpts/wang2022-\glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency}.md\#sec-4}.} The
practical sweet spot in the downstream literature is $N = 20$, which
captures most of the gain at half the compute of $N = 40$.

The result has not aged out. \citet[\S 2.3]{deepseek-r1} report that
DeepSeek-\glsterm{r1-zero}{R1-Zero} — an RL-trained \glsterm{reasoning-model}{reasoning model} that already lifts
AIME 2024 pass\@1 from $15.6\%$ to $77.9\%$ — gains a further
$8.8\%$ to $86.7\%$ when the same \glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency} rule is applied at
inference time over $64$ samples.\footnote{KB excerpt:
\texttt{kb/excerpts/deepseek-r1.md\#sec-2-3-aime}.} The mechanism that
worked on \glsterm{palm}{PaLM}-540B in 2022 still adds nearly nine points of pass\@1
to the strongest open-weight \glsterm{reasoning-model}{reasoning model} in 2025.

\subsection{The baseline interpretation}
\label{sec:sc-baseline}

\glsterm{self-consistency-2}{Self-consistency} is the floor of the \glsterm{test-time-compute}{test-time compute} axis
(\cref{sec:test-time-compute}). Every other strategy in this paper
adds either a verifier signal that re-weights the vote, a search
structure that reuses partial-prefix compute, or a training-time
intervention that changes the per-sample distribution itself:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Best-of-$N$ with outcome verifier} (\glsterm{outcome-reward-model}{ORM})
\citep[\S 2]{lightman2023-prm800k}: replaces the unweighted majority of
\cref{eq:self-consistency} with $\hat y = y^{(n^*)}$ where $n^* =
\argmax_n R_\phi(x, z^{(n)}, y^{(n)})$. Adds a learned signal at
training cost $C_\phi$ and runs only at fixed prompt overhead per
sample; the vote becomes weighted by verifier confidence.

\item \textbf{Best-of-$N$ with process \glsterm{reward-model}{reward model}} (\glsterm{process-reward-model}{PRM}, \cref{sec:process-reward-models}):
scores each \emph{step} of the trace and aggregates per-trace by min,
product, or last-step. \citet[\S 4]{lightman2023-prm800k} report \glsterm{process-reward-model}{PRM}
beats \glsterm{outcome-reward-model}{ORM} at matched $N$ on the MATH benchmark; the gap is largest
at small $N$, where a single bad-step penalty is fatal under
min-aggregation.

\item \textbf{Tree search with \glsterm{process-reward-model}{PRM}} (\cref{sec:tree-search}): replaces
flat sampling with MCTS or step-level beam search guided by a step
verifier \citep[\S 3]{rstar-math2025}. Reuses partial-prefix KV-cache
across siblings; cost scales as $B \times L$ rather than $N \times L$.

\item \textbf{RL on verifiable rewards} (\cref{sec:rlvr,sec:r1-family}):
trains the model so the per-sample distribution itself concentrates on
correct answers. \glsterm{self-consistency-2}{Self-consistency} over an \glsterm{rlvr-3}{RLVR}-trained model is then
the same algorithm operating on a sharper underlying distribution
\citep[\S 2.3]{deepseek-r1}.
\end{itemize}

The pattern is consistent: \glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency} is what every
test-time-compute paper compares against because it is what users get
``for free'' from any decoder once the model emits \glsterm{chain-of-thought}{CoT} traces. Outside
this paper's reasoning context, the same role of ``cheapest baseline''
makes \glsterm{self-consistency-2}{self-consistency} the standard calibration point for verifier-
and search-based methods at \cref{sec:process-reward-models} and
\cref{sec:tree-search}, and an inference-time control even on top of
the strongest \glsterm{rlvr-3}{RLVR}-trained models in \cref{sec:r1-family}.

\subsection{When self-consistency fails}
\label{sec:sc-when-fails}

Three failure modes are catalogued in the literature; each stresses a
different precondition of \cref{eq:self-consistency}.

\paragraph{Brittle answer extraction.} The vote in
\cref{eq:self-consistency} is over the \emph{extracted} answer
$y^{(n)}$, not the full trace $z^{(n)}$. If the regex fails — the
model omits the ``The answer is $X$'' template, or formats numbers
inconsistently (\textit{``42''} vs.\ \textit{``forty-two''} vs.\
\textit{``\$42$.$00$\$''}) — different traces arriving at the same
underlying answer can land in different vote bins, splitting the
correct cluster and causing a wrong answer to win by plurality. This
is the dominant production failure mode of CoT pipelines
\citep[\S 2.4]{wang2022-self-consistency}; mitigations are robust
parsers, structured-output decoders (Paper~1's inference-adjuncts
section on structured-output / FSM-constrained decoding), and
trained answer-format consistency.

\paragraph{Temperature too high or too low.}
\citet[\S 4.3]{wang2022-self-consistency} ablate $T$ and find an
inverted-U shape: at $T \to 0$ all $N$ samples collapse to the
greedy trace and the vote is degenerate; at large $T$ (e.g.,
$T \ge 1.2$) the per-sample correctness rate drops below the
threshold where majority recovers it. The gain band is roughly
$T \in [0.4, 0.9]$ on the reported benchmarks.

\paragraph{Many degenerate answer paths.} The mechanism assumes the
correct answer is the \emph{modal} extracted answer. This fails when
many traces converge on the same plausible-but-wrong answer — for
instance, on a word problem with a tempting incorrect arithmetic
shortcut, the model may produce that shortcut consistently across
samples and majority-vote into a wrong answer with high confidence.
\citet[\S 5]{wang2022-self-consistency} acknowledge that
self-consistency cannot recover from systematic error. Open-ended
generation (creative writing, summarisation, code with non-canonical
correct outputs) breaks the precondition that the answer space has
finite support; the analogue there is best-of-$N$ under a learned
verifier (\cref{sec:process-reward-models}), not majority vote.

\subsection{Forward link}
\label{sec:sc-forward}

Self-consistency moves the inference decoder from a single greedy draw
to $N$ independent samples plus a deterministic vote. The $N$ axis is
the simplest form of inference-time compute; the next section
(\cref{sec:test-time-compute}) generalises to the full taxonomy of
parallel-sampling, tree-search, and sequential-thinking compute
budgets, formalises the compute-optimal selection problem of
\citet{snell2024}, and presents the FLOPs-matched headline that on
medium-difficulty problems a $14\times$ smaller model with
test-time-compute beats the larger model at matched inference FLOPs.
Self-consistency is then re-derivable as the unweighted-majority
special case of that taxonomy at fixed sample count and verifier
weight $w_n = 1$.
