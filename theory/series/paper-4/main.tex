\title{
  The internal computation can be partially read: \\
  a method-pluralist account of LLM interpretability, 2016--2026
}
\author{Theory KB --- Paper 4 of 5}
\date{2026-05-04 (draft)}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Five interpretability methods --- logit/tuned lenses, probes, sparse
autoencoders, activation patching, and circuit tracing --- make
different commitments about what counts as a feature, a circuit, or
an explanation. Method-pluralism is principled, not a sign of
immaturity. Cross-method evidence converges in some cases (the IOI
circuit, factual-recall localization) and diverges in others
(``truth direction'' across datasets, SAE feature canonicality). We
walk the methods, the math each commits to, and the evidence pattern
we should expect to see at each scale.
\end{abstract}
