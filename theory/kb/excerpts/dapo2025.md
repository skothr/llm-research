# Excerpts — ByteDance Seed 2025, "DAPO"

## Abstract — four-technique improvement of GRPO {#abstract}

> Inference scaling empowers LLMs with unprecedented reasoning ability,
> with reinforcement learning as the core technique to elicit complex
> reasoning. […] [W]e introduce four key techniques of our algorithm that
> make large-scale LLM RL a success.

DAPO ("**D**ecoupled clip and dynamic s**A**mpling **P**olicy
**O**ptimization") trains Qwen2.5-32B with verifiable rewards on math,
reaching **50 points on AIME 2024** with the released training code,
data, and verl framework integration.

## §3 The four techniques {#sec-3}

The four innovations on top of GRPO (paper §3, Eq. 10–13):

### Clip-Higher (Eq. 10) {#sec-3-clip-higher}

PPO/GRPO use a symmetric clip range $[1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon]$. DAPO
decouples this:

$$
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{DAPO}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\frac{1}{G}\sum_i
\min\!\left(r_{i,t}(\theta) \hat{A}_i,\;
\mathrm{clip}(r_{i,t}(\theta), 1-\epsilon_{\mathrm{low}}, 1+\epsilon_{\mathrm{high}}) \hat{A}_i \right)\right] \tag{10}
$$

with $\epsilon_{\mathrm{high}} > \epsilon_{\mathrm{low}}$. The default
asymmetric setting is $\epsilon_{\mathrm{low}} = 0.2$ (unchanged from PPO)
and $\epsilon_{\mathrm{high}}$ enlarged (e.g. 0.28). The asymmetric clip
**permits larger probability increases for low-probability "exploration"
tokens** while still clipping aggressively in the downward direction —
this prevents collapse onto already-favored trajectories.

### Dynamic Sampling (§3.2) {#sec-3-dynamic-sampling}

When all $G$ trajectories in a group share the same reward (all 0 or all
1), the GRPO advantage is zero by group normalization and the sample
contributes no gradient. DAPO enforces the constraint

$$ 0 < |\{o_i : \mathrm{is\_equivalent}(a, o_i)\}| < G \tag{11} $$

i.e. each retained group must contain **both correct and incorrect
trajectories**. Groups failing this are discarded and re-sampled. This
concentrates compute on informative gradients.

### Token-Level Policy Gradient Loss (Eq. 12) {#sec-3-token-loss}

GRPO normalizes the per-trajectory loss by $1/|o_i|$, which (combined
with the outer $1/G$) means longer trajectories contribute proportionally
less per token. DAPO replaces this with a **token-aggregated loss**:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{DAPO}}(\theta) = -\frac{1}{\sum_{i=1}^G |o_i|} \sum_{i=1}^G \sum_{t=1}^{|o_i|} L_{i,t}(\theta) \tag{12}
$$

Every token is weighted equally regardless of trajectory length. This
avoids underweighting long correct CoTs.

### Overlong Reward Shaping (Eq. 13) {#sec-3-overlong}

Trajectories that hit the maximum generation length are usually
penalized as failures, but the failure signal is uninformative (the
model just ran out of budget). DAPO replaces the hard reward-zero with a
**length-based soft penalty**:

$$
R_{\mathrm{length}}(o_i) = \begin{cases} 0 & |o_i| \le L_{\mathrm{accept}} \\
\mathrm{linear\ interp.} & L_{\mathrm{accept}} < |o_i| < L_{\max} \\
-1 & |o_i| \ge L_{\max} \end{cases} \tag{13}
$$

This reduces variance from length-truncated samples while preserving
the signal that genuinely overlong reasoning is bad.

## §4 Empirical headline {#sec-4-headline}

> 50 points on AIME 2024 using Qwen2.5-32B base model.

Phase 1 sweep notes ~50% fewer training steps to a comparable score vs.
the GRPO baseline (DeepSeek-R1-Zero recipe applied to Qwen2.5-32B).
Available in the verl open-source framework with full training code +
curated dataset.
