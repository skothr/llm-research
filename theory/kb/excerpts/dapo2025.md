# Excerpts — ByteDance Seed 2025, "DAPO"

## Abstract — four-technique improvement of GRPO {#abstract}

> Inference scaling empowers LLMs with unprecedented reasoning ability,
> with reinforcement learning as the core technique to elicit complex
> reasoning. […] [W]e introduce four key techniques of our algorithm that
> make large-scale LLM RL a success.

DAPO ("**D**ecoupled clip and dynamic s**A**mpling **P**olicy
**O**ptimization") trains Qwen2.5-32B with verifiable rewards on math,
reaching **50 points on AIME 2024** with the released training code,
data, and verl framework integration.

## §2 The four techniques {#sec-2}

The four innovations on top of GRPO:

1. **Clip-Higher.** PPO/GRPO use a symmetric clip range $[1-\epsilon,
   1+\epsilon]$. DAPO decouples this to $[1-\epsilon_{\text{low}},
   1+\epsilon_{\text{high}}]$ with $\epsilon_{\text{high}} >
   \epsilon_{\text{low}}$, allowing larger upward updates when the policy
   already underestimates a high-reward action. Empirically this
   accelerates probability mass flowing to correct trajectories.

2. **Dynamic Sampling.** When all $G$ trajectories in a group share the
   same reward (all 0 or all 1), the GRPO advantage is zero by group
   normalization and the sample contributes no gradient. DAPO discards
   such groups and re-samples until each retained group contains both
   correct and incorrect trajectories. This concentrates compute on
   informative gradients.

3. **Token-Level Policy Gradient Loss.** GRPO normalizes the per-trajectory
   loss by $1/|o_i|$, which (combined with the outer $1/G$) means longer
   trajectories contribute proportionally less per token. DAPO replaces
   this with a token-aggregated loss: $\frac{1}{\sum_i |o_i|}\sum_i
   \sum_t L_{i,t}$, which weights every token equally regardless of
   trajectory length. This avoids underweighting long correct CoTs.

4. **Overlong Reward Shaping.** Trajectories that hit the maximum
   generation length are usually penalized as failures, but the failure
   signal is uninformative (the model just ran out of budget). DAPO
   applies a soft penalty proportional to overlongness rather than a hard
   reward zero, reducing variance from length-truncated samples.

## §3 Empirical headline {#sec-3-headline}

> 50 points on AIME 2024 using Qwen2.5-32B base model.

Phase 1 sweep notes ~50% fewer training steps to a comparable score vs.
the GRPO baseline (DeepSeek-R1-Zero recipe applied to Qwen2.5-32B).
Available in the verl open-source framework with full training code +
curated dataset.
